Membership Policy

| Membership Policies

  1. Membership is open to Bangladeshi individuals who passed the Secondary School Certificate (SSC) Examination in 1990, as well as corporate and donor members who meet specific Executive Committee conditions.
  2. Special cases may be considered for membership at the discretion of the Executive Committee.

  1. The club offers six categories of membership:
    • Founder Member
    • Donor Member
    • Permanent Member
    • Honorary Member
    • Corporate Member
    • General Member (Gold and Silver levels)
  2. Special cases may be considered for membership at the discretion of the Executive Committee.

  1. Entrance fees and monthly subscription amounts are set by the Executive Committee and may be adjusted in Extraordinary General Meetings.
  2. Membership dues are due by the seventh day of each month, with a 30-day grace period for payment.

  1. Only Founder and Permanent Members are entitled to voting rights, committee service, and the privilege of introducing guests to club events.
  2. Members in good standing may participate in club activities, events, and access all provided facilities.

  1. All membership applications must be submitted using the prescribed form, which must be supported by three Executive Committee members.
  2. The Executive Committee meets quarterly to review and approve new membership applications, with a maximum review period of six months.

  1. Members may voluntarily resign by written notice.
  2. Membership will be terminated if a member acts against club regulations, becomes insolvent, is deemed mentally unfit by a competent authority, or fails to pay dues for over a year.
  3. Upon a member's death, membership may be transferred to their child or grandchild, provided they meet eligibility criteria.

  1. Monthly membership fees are due within 30 days of invoicing. Reminders are issued if dues remain unpaid after the initial 30-day period.
  2. Failure to pay dues within the stipulated timeframe may result in suspension or termination of membership.

  1. Members who violate club rules or engage in conduct harmful to the club's reputation or finances may face disciplinary actions, including suspension or expulsion, as determined by the Executive Committee.

  1. Changes to the membership policy may be made by the Executive Committee during an Extraordinary General Meeting, with a three-week notice period provided to members. Amendments require a three-fourths majority vote for approval.
